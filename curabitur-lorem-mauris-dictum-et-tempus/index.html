ALL ABOUT DISASTER

November 2017 admin  
  • Gameplay
  • Difficulty
  • Effects
  • Graphics

Rampant, rampant, rampant. One word that describes disaster. Disaster is… disaster is very rampant nowadays. Ladies and gentlemen of the jury, I present to you the first ever chapter of my blog. The first page of three sixty. Witness as I go deeper down from left to right.

A part or measure also of reducing casualties of an earthquake is by making an evacuation plan or asking the captain of your barangay for their official evacuation plan for the barangay and spreading the word

Okay so much for that, let’s be quick with this first chapter of my blog because who the hail would want a long and boring blog anyways? Let us of course start by giving a quick definition of disaster and end with a deeper meaning of it, following a quick overview of what my day actually looked like listening to my good ol teacher.

What is a disaster? According to Galapon’s powerpoint (2017), “Disaster is either natural or man-made events wherein communities experience severe danger and incur loss of lives and properties causing disruption in its social structure....”. Oh my god ryan, what the hail does that mean anyway? Let me explain. There are two types of disaster my friend, it can be either natural (caused by nature itself like floods, typhoons, storm surges, earthquake etc.) or man-made (caused by man or involving human intervention like fire, sea, and air accidents, terrorism, oil spills etc.). Any disaster that is not natural and man-made is not a disaster. But if these two types of disaster are combined then it will be called a combination.

Moving on from differentiating the types of disaster. Let us cite two examples of a natural disaster that you might still be confused of. Of course you’ve already heard of tsunamis and tidal waves right? But do you know what their differences are? Although both are sea waves, they are actually different and unrelated to each other. Tidal wave would be the shallow water wave caused by the gravitational interaction between the sun, moon and the earth whereas tsunami would be the sea wave caused by an underwater earthquake or landslide displacing the ocean water.

Okay so let’s move to the other type of a natural disaster, the earthquake. From this time around, you might be thinking “why? It’s only the first day of classes, why are there so many topics?”, yes of course you might be thinking that, but let me tell you that I don’t actually care. So earthquakes, whoo exciting isn’t it? The first thing that popped up in my head when I the first time I heard the word earthquake is “what’s an earthquake?”. Okay brothers and sisters, from the sources I have gathered and from our dearest teacher, I have come up with a delicious and short definition of the meaning of an earthquake. Earthquake, simply put, is a shake. But if you are some kind of a nerd, the definition of an earthquake would be the result of a sudden release of energy in the Earth’s crust that create seismic waves.

Ryan, I thought you said that this blog would be short and quick? Well, my friend, a wise man once said “there are no shortcuts in life”. Do not worry, the class is about to end, we are now in the phases of mitigating an earthquake. To make the story short, there are 6 phases of mitigating an earthquake. Phase 1, phase 2, phase 3, phase 4, phase 5, and phase 6. Okay next topic would be… “wait ryaan, tell me more about these phases” Aha! for a second I thought you want this to be short but since you insist , sure I can give you more details if you want.

Phase 1 or Alarm (this is where the siren or bell rings for a minute, this also means that an ongoing ground shaking is ofcourse ongoing”. Phase 2 would be Response (this is during the quake, in this phase you are expected to perform the duck cover and hold), next would be the Phase 3 or Evacuation phase (this is after the shaking, all building occupants evacuate, following pre-determined routes, to the evacuation areas or open spaces) Phase 4 would be the Assembly part (at the desisnated evacuation area, the evacuees must be grouped together. And then there’s the Head Count or the fifth phase (team leaders should check and make sure all evacuees are present). Lastly Phase 6 or the Evaluation phase (this is where you evaluate the conduction of drill, and identify the problems in order to improve the drill in the future).

So now you know how to mitigate earthquakes, how about if the earthquake is happening like right now. My friend, there is one important thing that you need to remember if an ongoing earthquake is happening, protect yourself! Do those things that you learned from the last topic I discussed about mitigating an earthquake, especially the DUCK COVER AND HOLD part.

How about after an earthquake? There are only five things you need to remember to fully understand the topic, I mean to quickly understand the topic. DON’T RUN. DON’T PUSH. DON’T TALK. DON’T RETURN. DON’T BRING YOUR THINGS!

A part or measure also of reducing casualties of an earthquake is by making an evacuation plan or asking the captain of your barangay for their official evacuation plan for the barangay and spreading the word

“I can’t take this anymore, there’s just too much sub-topics to learn in single day”. Well you know what, learning too much is better than learning too many. I would be amazed if you are still reading this part of the chapter (hope you’re not actually but glad you made it this far). I think you already have an idea of what natural disaster is, let’s move on to the man-made disaster. This part, I promise, would be the shortest. Man-made disaster are disasters caused by man or involving human intervention. There is only one example of disaster that would be discussed here, and that is fire. “What’s a fire” No no no, let us not go there anymore, let’s skip to the most common causes of fire because I am following the powerpoint (to be honest). The causes would be overheating appliances, overloaded circuits, neglected rubbish or grass fire, leaking lpg, and unattended cooking and candles. Simply put, if you do not want your house to burn, well avoid these things as much as possible.

Quick wasn’t it? Hope you learned something from my blog. This is, Ryan Tillaman, signing off.
